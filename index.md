# **About Me**

👋 Hi! I am a **Ph.D. Student** in the Department of Artificial Intelligence at **Korea University**. My academic journey is driven by a passion for developing advanced AI systems that bridge the gap between computational models and real-world medical or robotic applications. Currently, I am a member of the **[Medical Artificial Intelligence (MAI) Lab](http://mailab.korea.ac.kr/)**, advised by **Prof. [Tae-Eui Kam](https://scholar.google.com/citations?user=y7B2OsUAAAAJ&hl=en)**.

My recent research focuses on how **Reinforcement Learning (RL)** and **Generative Models** can be utilized to optimize brain network analysis and autonomous diagnostic systems. Furthermore, I am particularly interested in enhancing **Medical Diagnosis** through Functional Connectivity (FC) and Graph Neural Networks (GNNs). Recently, I have also expanded my research interests to include **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models**, aiming to bridge multimodal perception with generalized actionable intelligence across diverse environments.

## **Research Interests**

My research sits at the intersection of reinforcement learning, brain disease diagnosis, agentic systems, and vision-language-action model.
* **Reinforcement Learning (RL):** Focusing on formulating sequential decision-making problems across diverse domains and applying reinforcement learning to resolve complex, high-dimensional challenges through effective optimization strategies.
* **Brain Disease Diagnosis:** Utilizing Resting state-functional MRI (rs-fMRI) based static and dynamic functional connectivity features to diagnose conditions like Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) and Major Depressive Disorder (MDD).
* **Graph Neural Networks (GNNs):** Leveraging brain topology-driven graph structure learning for personalized medical applications.
* **Generative Models:** Exploring Diffusion Transformers and other generative architectures for brain network generation and augmentation.
* **Agentic AI** Developing autonomous agentic systems designed for clinical diagnosis by formulating specialized reasoning paths that interpret complex functional connectivity networks.
* **Vision-Language-Action (VLA) Models:** Exploring the integration of multimodal perception with generalized actionable intelligence to develop embodied AI capable of reasoning and acting across diverse environments.
